# First-Principles Chemistry

## Atomic Structure and Bonding

Atomic number $Z$ = protons; mass number $A$ = protons + neutrons. Electron configuration determines
valence and preferred oxidation states. Bonding extremes — ionic, covalent, metallic — are limits of
a continuum described by electronegativity differences and orbital overlap. Bond dissociation enthalpies
and bond orders quantify strength for thermochemical bookkeeping.

## Stoichiometry and the Ideal Gas

Moles: $n=m/M$. Concentration: $C=n/V$.
$$PV=nRT,\qquad R=8.314\,\mathrm{J\,mol^{-1}\,K^{-1}}.$$
Good approximation at moderate $P,T$; real-gas corrections (van der Waals, virial) matter near
condensation and at high pressure.

## Thermochemistry and Spontaneity

$\Delta H = H_{\mathrm{products}}-H_{\mathrm{reactants}}$. **Hess's law:** enthalpy is a state function,
so $\Delta H_{\mathrm{total}}=\sum\Delta H_{\mathrm{steps}}$.

**Gibbs free energy:** $\Delta G=\Delta H-T\Delta S$. At constant $T,P$, processes with $\Delta G<0$
are spontaneous. Standard relation to equilibrium constant:
$$\Delta G^\circ=-RT\ln K \quad\Rightarrow\quad K=e^{-\Delta G^\circ/RT}.$$
Temperature dependence of $K$ follows van 't Hoff: $d\ln K/dT=\Delta H^\circ/RT^2$.

## Chemical Equilibrium

For $aA+bB\rightleftharpoons cC+dD$:
$$K_{eq}=\frac{[C]^c[D]^d}{[A]^a[B]^b}.$$
**Le Chatelier's principle:** equilibrium shifts to counteract imposed changes. Coupled equilibria
(solubility, acid–base, complexation) share species and must be solved simultaneously in quantitative work.

## Reaction Kinetics

Rate law: $\mathrm{rate}=k[A]^m[B]^n$.
**Arrhenius:** $k=A e^{-E_a/RT}$ — exponential sensitivity to activation energy and temperature.
Collision theory and transition-state theory connect $A$ and $E_a$ to molecular details. Diffusion
limits and mass-transport constraints appear in heterogeneous catalysis and electrochemical cells.

## Electrochemistry

**Nernst equation:** $E=E^\circ-\frac{RT}{nF}\ln Q$. Cell potential relates to $\Delta G=-nFE$.
Overpotential, exchange current density, and Butler–Volmer kinetics govern real electrode rates beyond
equilibrium thermodynamics — essential for batteries, electrolyzers, and corrosion science.

## Acid–Base

$pH=-\log[H^+]$, $pH+pOH=14$ at 25°C. Weak acid: $K_a=[H^+][A^-]/[HA]$. Polyprotic acids and
ampholytes require speciation diagrams for accurate $pH$ prediction.
