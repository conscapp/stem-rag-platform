# Nanotechnology: Nanoscale Materials (First Principles)

## Surface-to-Volume Ratio

For a sphere of radius $r$: surface area $A = 4\pi r^2$, volume $V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$.

$$\frac{A}{V} = \frac{3}{r}$$

As $r \to$ nm scale, surface area dominates — reactivity, catalysis, and toxicity differ from bulk material [chemistry].

## Quantum Confinement (Semiconductor Nanoparticles)

Band gap increases as particle size decreases below exciton Bohr radius:

$$E_g(\text{nano}) > E_g(\text{bulk})$$

Optical absorption/emission wavelength shifts with size — tunable for sensors and photovoltaics [physics + chemistry].

## Stoichiometry at Nanoscale

Mole balance $n = m/M$ [chemistry] applies; at nm scale, discrete atom counts matter. A 3 nm gold nanoparticle has ~1000 atoms — statistical fluctuations affect properties.

## Colloidal Stability

Gibbs free energy $\Delta G = \Delta H - T\Delta S$ [chemistry] governs aggregation. Surface functionalization changes $\Delta H$ of particle-particle interaction.

## Mechanical Properties

Young's modulus $E = \sigma/\epsilon$ [engineering] of nanostructures can exceed bulk values but defect density increases. Factor of safety still applies at device scale.
