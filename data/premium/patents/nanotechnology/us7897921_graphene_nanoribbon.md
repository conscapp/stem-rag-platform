# Field of invention

Semiconductor devices using graphene nanoribbons with controlled edge chirality for field-effect transistor channels.

# Representative claims (paraphrased for retrieval)

1. A transistor comprising a graphene nanoribbon channel wherein edge structure is controlled to produce a bandgap suitable for switching at room temperature.
2. A method of fabricating nanoribbon channels on an insulating substrate with source and drain contacts aligned to ribbon axis.
3. The device of claim 1 wherein ribbon width is below 50 nm.

# Prior art context documented in prosecution

- Early graphene FETs exhibited ambipolar behavior and zero bandgap.
- Carbon nanotube transistors faced chirality control challenges at scale.
- Lithographic patterning of graphene introduced edge disorder affecting mobility.
