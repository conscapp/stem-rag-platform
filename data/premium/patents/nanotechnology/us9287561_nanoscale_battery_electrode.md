# Field of invention

Energy storage electrodes using nanostructured active materials (silicon nanowires, porous carbon scaffolds) to accommodate volume expansion during lithium insertion.

# Representative claim themes (paraphrased)

1. An anode comprising nanoscale silicon structures on a conductive scaffold configured to expand without pulverization during lithiation.
2. A method of forming porous carbon hosts with controlled pore size distribution for high-capacity anodes.
3. The electrode of claim 1 wherein capacity retention exceeds a stated cycle threshold under defined C-rate.

# Prior art problems encoded in patents

- Bulk silicon anodes crack from ~300% volume change.
- Thin-film silicon lacks areal capacity for practical cells.
- SEI growth on high-surface-area nanostructures can consume electrolyte.
